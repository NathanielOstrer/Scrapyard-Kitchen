Beer, Flour

Florence Fabricant 1 large loaf Time40 minutes

Ingredients

  • Cornmeal
  • 3 cups flour (See note)
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 12-ounce can beer, the blander the better
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Butter a 9-by-5-by-3-inch loaf pan and dust with cornmeal. For stovetop baking use a heavy Dutch oven or similar pot that has been greased and dusted with cornmeal but not preheated.
  2. Mix flour, baking powder, salt and sugar and stir in the beer. Placebatter in the pan. Oven baking will take about 35 minutes. For stovetop baking, cover the pan and place it on a heat shield over a low to medium flame and bake until a knife or cake tester comes out clean.
  3. Remove from pan, drizzle with butter if desired and allow to cool completely before slicing. The bread has a texture similar to that of English muffins and tastes best toasted.

Tip

  • Three cups of self-rising flour can be substituted. Omit the baking powder and salt.
