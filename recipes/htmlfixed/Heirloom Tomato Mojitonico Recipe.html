Gin, Tomato

Jonathan Miles 1 drink Time5 minutes

Steve Kagan for The New York Times

Ingredients

  • Kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper, for rim 1/2 lime, cut into sections
  • ½ cup heirloom tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh herbs (like basil, tarragon, thyme, chives or mint) loosely packed and not chopped
  • 1 ½ ounces gin
  • 2 ounces tonic water, or to taste

Preparation

  1. Rim a 16-ounce glass by moistening the edge with a lime wedge, then dipping the glass into a small plate filled with equal parts salt and pepper. Into the same glass add the tomatoes, herbs and lime sections, then muddle, gently, until the mixture is equal parts juice and solids. Add ice, stir, then add the gin, and stir again. Top with the tonic water.
