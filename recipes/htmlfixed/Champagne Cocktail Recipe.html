Bartlett Pear, Champagne, Pear Liqueur

William L. Hamilton 1 serving

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon fresh Bartlett pear, cut into 1/4-inch dice and macerated in pear liqueur, to cover (Craft uses Belle de Brillet.)
  • 5 ounces Champagne

Preparation

  1. Peel, seed and dice 1 pear into equal 1/4-inch cubes. Place the cubes in a plastic container, and pour in enough pear liqueur to cover. Let the pear macerate in the refrigerator for 2 hours (not more, or the cubes will become oversaturated). If the pear is properly macerated, the cubes will dance in the glass.
  2. When ready to serve, spoon about 1 teaspoon of macerated pear into the bottom of a Champagne flute. Pour Champagne slowly into the flute (Craft uses a Riedel Restaurant Series glass, with more of a wineglass shape), because it tends to bubble up in reaction to the pears. Serve.
