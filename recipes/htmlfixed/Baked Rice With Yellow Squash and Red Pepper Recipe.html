Vegetarian, Red Bell Pepper, Rice, Yellow Squash

Pierre Franey 4 servings Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-size red sweet pepper
  • 2 small yellow squash, about 1/2 pound
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½ cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 cup converted rice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme or 1/2 teaspoon dried
  • ⅛ teaspoon Tabasco sauce
  • 1 ¼ cups water or fresh or canned chicken broth
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Core and seed pepper. Cut flesh into thin strips about 1 1/2 inches long.
  2. Wash and trim squash. Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon of the butter in a saucepan and add onion. Cook, stirring, until wilted. Add pepper strips and squash and cook, stirring, 1 minute over medium heat. Add rice, bay leaf, thyme, Tabasco sauce, water, salt and pepper. Stir and bring to a boil. Cover and simmer for 17 minutes.
  4. Discard thyme sprigs and bay leaf. Using a fork, distribute the remaining 1 tablespoon butter through the rice. Keep covered in a warm place until ready to serve.
