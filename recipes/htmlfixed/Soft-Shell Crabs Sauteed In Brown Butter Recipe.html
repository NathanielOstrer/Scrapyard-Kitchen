Butter, Crab, Milk, New England

Molly O'Neill Four servings Time35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 teaspoons Tabasco
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • ½ teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 8 soft-shell crabs, cleaned
  • 8 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup lemon juice
  • ½ cup parsley, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ground pepper, plus more to taste

Preparation

  1. Combine the milk and Tabasco in a bowl. Mix the mustard powder, cayenne pepper, white pepper, salt, sugar and flour in a shallow pan. Dip the crabs in the milk and dredge in flour mixture. Heat 2 tablespoons of the butter in a large saute pan over medium-high heat until bubbling. Place the crabs in the pan and cook for 3 minutes. Turn and cook until tender, about 2 more minutes. Drain on paper towels.
  2. Add 1/2 cup lemon juice to pan and cook over high heat until it glazes the pan. Stir in remaining butter and cook over medium heat until brown, about 5 minutes. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Take the pan off the heat. Whisk in the remaining 1/2 cup lemon juice and parsley. Season with salt and pepper, pour over crabs and serve.
