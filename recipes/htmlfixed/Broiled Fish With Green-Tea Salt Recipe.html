Green Tea, Halibut, Sesame Oil

Mark Bittman 4 servings Time15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ to 2 pounds halibut, bluefish or mackerel fillets
  • 1 tablespoon dark sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon powdered green tea
  • 1 ½ teaspoons coarse salt
  • 1 lime, quartered

Preparation

  1. Preheat broiler. Arrange fish on a broiling pan, skin side down, and brush lightly with sesame oil. Mix together tea and salt.
  2. Place pan on broiler rack adjusted so that fish is as close as possible to heating element, as close as 2 inches away. Broil, checking fish once or twice to make sure it is browning but not burning; lower rack if necessary. When fish is browned and a thin-bladed knife penetrates it with little resistance, it is done. Total cooking time in a good broiler will be about 5 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle fish liberally with the tea-salt mixture and serve with lime wedges.
