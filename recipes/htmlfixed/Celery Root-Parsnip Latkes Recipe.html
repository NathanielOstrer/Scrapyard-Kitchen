Melissa Clark About 4 dozen latkes Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 pound celery root, peeled and grated
  • 1 pound parsnips, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and grated
  • 1 ¼ cups matzo meal
  • ¾ cup chopped Italian parsley
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt, more for serving
  • ¾ teaspoon cracked black pepper
  • Safflower oil

Preparation

  1. Place grated celery root, parsnips and onion in a large bowl. Sprinkle in matzo meal and toss mixture together with your hands. Add parsley, eggs, salt and pepper and combine again using your hands until ingredients are incorporated.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large sauté pan over medium-high. Take a heaping tablespoon of the mixture and flatten between your palms. Fry latkes, without moving them, for 4 to 5 minutes, checking that they don’t over-brown. (You should be able to fry them in batches of 11 to 12, depending on pan size.) Flip latkes, turn heat down to medium-low and fry another 4 minutes, or until well browned and tender. Transfer to a baking sheet lined with paper towels. Sprinkle with additional salt. Serve warm.
