Ham, Squid, Tomato

Moira Hodgson 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 4 squid of 1/2 pound each
  • 3 to 4 ounces smoked ham
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, peeled (or one cup canned Italian)
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • About 4 tablespoons fresh chopped parsley
  • 1 medium to large onion
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic

Preparation

  1. Clean the squid. Chop the tentacles finely with the ham and tomatoes and add salt, pepper and chopped parsley to make a thick mixture. Stuff the squid with this, but not quite full and fasten each with a toothpick.
  2. Chop the onion and cook it gently in the oil with the whole cloves of garlic. Add the squid and cook them gently over a low heat for 20 minutes, adding a little water (or white wine) toward the end if the sauce seems to be drying up.
  3. Serve on a bed of rice.
