Cheese, Lettuce, Italian

Pierre Franey 4 servings Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 heads romaine lettuce, about 2 pounds
  • 2 ounces cheese, preferably Gruyere although Swiss may be used
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ⅓ cup finely chopped onion
  • Salt to taste if desired
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream

Preparation

  1. Place the romaine, one head at a time, on a flat surface. Slice off the end about 3 inches from the bottom and discard. Cut the remaining lettuce crosswise into 2-inch pieces. If necessary, rinse the lettuce thoroughly and drain well.
  2. Bring to a boil enough water to cover the lettuce. Add lettuce. When the water returns to a boil let simmer 8 minutes. Drain well. Rinse under cold water and drain well.
  3. Put the lettuce in the container of a food processor or electric blender and process until finely ground. Scrape the mixture into a colander and drain well. Discard the liquid. There should be about 3 1/2 cups of ground lettuce.
  4. Grate the cheese, using fine blade of grater. Set aside.
  5. Preheat the broiler to high.
  6. Heat butter in a saucepan and add onion. Cook, stirring, until wilted. Add ground lettuce, salt and pepper to taste. Add cream. Cook, stirring, until piping hot.
  7. Spoon mixture into a small baking dish. Sprinkle with the cheese. Place the dish under the broiler until the cheese is melted and the top is golden brown, about 5 minutes.
