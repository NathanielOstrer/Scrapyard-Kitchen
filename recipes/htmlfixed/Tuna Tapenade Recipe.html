Martha Rose Shulman Makes about 1 1/4 cups

Andrew Scrivani for The New York Times

Ingredients

  • ¼ pound imported black olives, pitted
  • 2 garlic cloves, halved, green shoots removed
  • 1 ½ tablespoons capers, drained and rinsed thoroughly with cold waterrinsed
  • 2-4 anchovy fillets (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 can olive-oil packed light (not albacore) tuna
  • Freshly ground pepper

Preparation

  1. Make sure that there are no pits mixed in with your olives.
  2. Turn on a food processor fitted with the steel blade and drop in the garlic. When finely chopped and adhering to the sides of the machine, stop the machine and scrape down the sides of the bowl. Add the remaining ingredients except the tuna and puree to a smooth paste. Scrape down the sides of the bowl and add the tuna. Process until you have a smooth paste. Add freshly ground pepper to taste. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate, covered, until ready to use.
