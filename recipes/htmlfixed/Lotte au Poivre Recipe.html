Monkfish, French

Pierre Franey 4 servings Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 fillets monkfish, about 1 1/4 pounds
  • 2 teaspoons crushed white peppercorns, or coarse black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped shallots
  • 4 tablespoons dry white wine
  • 4 tablespoons soft butter
  • Chervil or dill sprigs for garnish

Preparation

  1. Coat the fish on both sides with the pepper. Sprinkle with salt to taste.
  2. Heat the oil in a black cast-iron skillet over a medium flame. Add the fish immediately and cook for about 2 minutes until lightly browned. Turn the filets, cover tightly, lower the heat and cook for 3 minutes. Transfer the fish to a warm plate and keep warm.
  3. Add the shallots to the skillet; stir and cook over a medium heat until wilted. Add wine and bring to a boil, stirring. Reduce by half, add butter and blend well. Spoon the sauce over each fillet. Garnish with a sprig of chervil or dill.
