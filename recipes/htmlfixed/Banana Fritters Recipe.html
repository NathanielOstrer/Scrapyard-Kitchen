Vegetarian, Nut Free, Banana, Egg, Flour

Jacques Pepin 6 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 ⅓ cups ice-cold water
  • 1 cup (approximately) canola oil
  • 3 ripe bananas
  • ½ cup (approximately) granulated or confectioner's sugar

Preparation

  1. Place the flour, egg and about half the water in a bowl. Mix with a whisk for a few seconds, until the mixture is smooth. Whisk in the remainder of the water. If you are not completing the recipe immediately, refrigerate the batter until you are ready to make the fritters.
  2. In a large nonstick skillet, heat 1/4 cup of the oil to about 400 degrees. Meanwhile, peel the bananas and, holding them directly over the bowl containing the batter, cut them crosswise into 1/2-inch slices and let the slices fall into the batter.
  3. Make 3 or 4 fritters at a time, using about 1/4 cup of the batter for each and pouring it into the hot oil. Spread the mixture lightly to create disks about 3 inches in diameter. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes, turn and cook for 2 to 3 minutes on the other side, until the fritters are nicely browned.
  4. Remove the fritters with a slotted spoon and place them on a wire rack to drain. Continue making fritters, adding more oil as needed, until all the batter has been used. Sprinkle the drained fritters generously with sugar and serve immediately.
