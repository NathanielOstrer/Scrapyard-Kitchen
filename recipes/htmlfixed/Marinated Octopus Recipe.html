Gelatin, Lemon Juice, Octopus, Italian

Maura Egan About 6 servings Time3 hours 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds octopus
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 6 tablespoons fresh lemon juice, and 2 tablespoons extra if using gelatin
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped salted capers, plus extra for sprinkling
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 envelope unflavored gelatin
  • Sprigs fresh oregano
  • ½ cup black olives
  • ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes in oil

Preparation

  1. Wash the octopus inside and out in cold water and place in a large kettle. Add the bay leaves and 1 tablespoon salt and cover with cold water. Heat to boiling over medium-high flame. Simmer over medium heat until the thick part of a tentacle can easily be sliced, about 40 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and cool the octopus in the water for 1 hour.
  2. While the octopus cools, prepare the marinade: combine the olive oil, 6 tablespoons lemon juice, parsley, chopped capers, 1/2 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon pepper in a bowl. Whisk until creamy and set aside.
  3. Lift the octopus into a colander in a sink and rinse each tentacle under cold water while rubbing off the skin and suckers. Cut off the tentacles and reserve; turn the body inside out and rinse well.
  4. Dissolve the gelatin in 1/2 cup boiling water and stir in 2 tablespoons lemon juice. Stir in 1/2 cup cold water. Place the tentacles of one octopus in a rectangular terrine and pour the gelatin mixture over it. Refrigerate until set, about an hour.
  5. Slice the remaining tentacles and bodies into bite-size pieces and place them in the bowl of marinade. Toss to coat. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  6. To serve, invert molded tentacles onto a platter and slice. Arrange the bowl of marinated octopus alongside, garnish with the oregano and extra capers. Serve with olives and sun-dried tomatoes.
