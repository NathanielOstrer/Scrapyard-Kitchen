Chicken, Wild Rice, French

Craig Claiborne And Pierre Franey 10 to 12 servings Time2 hours 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups raw wild rice
  • 3 cups cooked chicken, cut into cubes
  • 5 tablespoons butter
  • 5 tablespoons flour
  • 3 ½ cups rich chicken broth
  • ¾ pound thinly sliced mushrooms (about 4 cups)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped shallots
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper

Preparation

For the wild rice

  1. Rinse and drain each cup of wild rice until water runs clear.
  2. Put the rice in a saucepan or casserole and, for each cup of rice, add three cups of water and salt to taste.
  3. Bring to the boil and cover.
  4. Simmer 45 minutes to one hour or until the rice grains are puffed open. If the grains are not puffed after an hour, remove the pan from the heat and let stand until the grains open.
  5. Drain well. Use for stuffings or toss with butter and serve as a side dish. (Yield: Five cups for each cup of raw rice.)

For the casserole

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Melt four tablespoons of the butter in a saucepan and add the flour, stirring with a wire whisk. When blended, add the broth, stirring rapidly with the whisk. Let simmer 15 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, melt the remaining one tablespoon butter in a heavy saucepan and add the mushrooms and lemon juice. Cook, stirring often, until mushrooms give up their liquid. Add the shallots and continue cooking until most of the liquid evaporates.
  4. Add the mushrooms to the sauce. Add the cream, nutmeg, salt and pepper to taste. Continue cooking about 15 minutes.
  5. In a baking casserole, add the sauce, rice and chicken and blend. Cover loosely and bake 45 minutes.
