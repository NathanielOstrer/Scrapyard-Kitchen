Guajillo Chile, Jalapeno, Tomatillo, Southwestern

Sam Sifton Serves 6-8 Time20 minutes

Peden & Munk for The New York Times. Food stylist: Maggie Ruggiero. Prop stylist: Lauren Smith Ford.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-size onions, peeled and halved
  • 8 tomatillos, husks removed
  • 4 jalapeño peppers
  • 4 dried guajillo chiles
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, tender part only, peeled, halved and thinly sliced into half-moons
  • 1 tablespoon palm or light-brown sugar
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons lime juice

Preparation

  1. Set broiler to high or light an outdoor grill. Cook the onions, tomatillos and jalapeños under or over the heat until they are charred, then set aside. Meanwhile, toast the guajillo chiles on a lower rack of the oven or off to the side of the grill, turning occasionally, 5 to 8 minutes.
  2. Crumble guajillos into the bowl of a food processor, and pulse a few times to create crumbs. Add the jalapeños, then the garlic, lemongrass and palm sugar, and pulse a few times to combine. Add the onions, then the tomatillos, and run the machine until you have a thick salsa.
  3. Add fish sauce and lime juice a few tablespoons at a time, adjusting the taste as you go. Covered, the jaew will keep in the refrigerator for a few days.
