Apple, Pear

Emily Weinstein 6 servings Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • ½ cup honey
  • ⅓ cup sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • Zest and juice of 1/2 orange, zest removed with a vegetable peeler cut in wide strips
  • Zest and juice of 1/2 lemon, zest removed with a vegetable peeler cut in wide strips
  • 2 pieces star anise
  • 1 piece thin cinnamon stick (about 1½ inches long)
  • 1 piece vanilla bean (about 2 inches long), split, seeds removed with a paring knife, seeds and bean reserved and scraped
  • 3 medium apples or pears, peeled, halved, and cored

Preparation

  1. Put all the ingredients except the fruit in a large saucepan, turn the heat to medium-high, and bring to a boil. As soon as the liquid boils, reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer the syrup for 5 minutes.
  2. Carefully drop the apples or pears into the pan and bring the syrup back to a simmer. Cover the pan and cook until the fruit can be pierced easily with a thin knife, 10 to 15 minutes, depending on the fruit; check early and often. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the apples or pears to a bowl.
  3. Turn up the heat and boil the syrup for another 10 minutes, at which point you’ll have about 1¼ cups. Pour the syrup over the fruit, cover, and let cool until slightly warm or at room temperature.

Tip

  • For storing: You can make the fruit up to a day ahead. Pack the fruit and syrup into a covered container and refrigerate.
