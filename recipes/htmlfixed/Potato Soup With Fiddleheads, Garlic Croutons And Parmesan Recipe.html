Chicken Broth, Fiddlehead Fern, Potato

Molly O'Neill Four servings Time15 minutes

Ingredients

The soup:

  • 3 medium baking potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth, homemade or low-sodium canned
  • ¼ cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste

The garnish:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 6 thick slices stale Italian bread, cut into 1/4-inch cubes
  • ¾ cup trimmed and cleaned fiddleheads, blanched until tender
  • 4 tablespoons shaved Parmesan cheese

Preparation

  1. Cook the potatoes in water until tender. Drain and pass through a ricer. Place in a medium-size saucepan over medium heat and stir in the chicken broth and cream. Season with salt and pepper and cook until heated through.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a medium-size skillet. Add the garlic and cook a few seconds. Add the bread cubes and cook until golden and crisp. Ladle the soup among 4 bowls. Garnish with fiddleheads, croutons and Parmesan shavings. Serve immediately.
