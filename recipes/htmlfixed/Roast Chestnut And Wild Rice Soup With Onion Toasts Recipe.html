Chestnut, Wild Rice

R. W. Apple Jr.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound chestnuts
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 2 medium onions, diced
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic
  • 3 shallots, finely chopped
  • 1 ½ cups cooked wild rice
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 quarts chicken stock or canned broth
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves (removed from stems), chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Using a sharp knife and a kitchen mallet, lightly tap an X into the flat side of each chestnut. Place chestnuts in a single layer on a baking sheet, cut side up. Roast until the shells begin to peel back, about 15 minutes. Cool, peel and set aside.
  2. In a 6-quart soup pot over low heat, melt butter until it foams. Add onion, and saute until translucent, about 3 minutes. Add garlic and shallots; saute 2 more minutes.
  3. Add chestnuts, wild rice, bay leaves and stock to pot. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until chestnuts are tender, about 15 minutes. Add cream, return to a simmer and remove from heat.
  4. Add thyme, and salt and pepper to taste. Using a blender, puree half of the soup. Return puree to the pot, adjust seasonings, and serve topped with onion toasts.
