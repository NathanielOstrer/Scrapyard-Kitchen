Vegetarian, Vegan, Eggplant, Tomato, Russian

William Grimes 3 cups

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant (about 2 pounds)
  • 1 cup finely chopped onions
  • 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ cup finely chopped green pepper
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
  • 2 large tomatoes, peeled, seeded and finely chopped
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • Black pepper
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Dark rye or pumpernickel or sesame-seed crackers, for serving.

Preparation

  1. Heat the oven to 425 degrees. Bake the eggplant on a baking sheet in the center of the oven, turning it over once or twice, until it is soft and its skin is charred and blistered, about 1 hour.
  2. Meanwhile, in a skillet, cook the onions in 4 tablespoons oil over medium heat until they are soft but not brown, 6 to 8 minutes. Stir in the green pepper and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes longer. With a rubber spatula, scrape the contents of the skillet into a mixing bowl.
  3. Remove the skin from the baked eggplant with a small, sharp knife, then chop the eggplant pulp finely, almost to a pure. Add it to the mixing bowl and stir in the tomatoes, sugar, salt and a few grindings of black pepper. Mix together thoroughly. Heat the remaining 2 tablespoons oil in the skillet over moderate heat and pour in the eggplant mixture. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, then turn the heat to low, cover the skillet, and simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Uncover and cook 30 minutes, stirring from time to time, until all the moisture in the pan has evaporated and the mixture is thick enough to hold its shape in a spoon. Stir in 2 tablespoons lemon juice and taste for seasoning, adding salt, pepper and lemon juice to taste. Transfer the “caviar” to a mixing bowl and chill, covered with plastic wrap, until ready to serve. Serve on squares of bread or on sesame-seed crackers.
