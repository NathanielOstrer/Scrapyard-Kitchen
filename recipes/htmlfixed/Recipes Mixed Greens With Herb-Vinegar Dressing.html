Raw, Vegetarian, Balsamic Vinegar, Mixed Green, Red Wine, Italian

Amanda Hesser 4 servings

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
  • ¼ cup red-wine vinegar
  • ¼ cup packed fresh herbs (a mixture of basil, parsley, thyme, rosemary and oregano; it's not necessary to use all of them)
  • About 1/4 cup peanut oil
  • 2 shallots, peeled and sliced
  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Coarse sea salt or kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 handfuls young lettuces (a mixture of peppercress, arugula, frisée, mizuna, red oak or any other kinds you like)
  • ¼ cup fresh chervil and tarragon leaves

Preparation

  1. Three days before, combine the vinegars with the herb mixture in a jar. Seal and let infuse in the refrigerator for three days.
  2. Strain the herbs from the vinegar and pour the vinegar into a saucepan. Place over high heat and reduce by half. Fill another saucepan with 1/4 inch of peanut oil. Heat the oil to 170 degrees, then add the shallots. Remove from the heat and cool. Strain. Place the vinegar in a bowl, then gradually whisk in the shallot oil and then the olive oil until emulsified. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  3. In a large bowl, mix together the lettuces and the chervil and tarragon. Sprinkle with about 3 tablespoons of the dressing and toss gently and thoroughly, until all of the leaves are thinly coated. Add a little more dressing if necessary, but the delicate leaves should not be weighed down with it. Season with more salt and pepper if needed. Serve.
