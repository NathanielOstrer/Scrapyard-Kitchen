Vegetarian, Vegan, Dairy Free, Gluten Free, Nut Free, Jalapeno, Pinto Bean, Tomato, Mexican

Craig Claiborne 6 servings TimeAbout 1 hour 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 ¼ cups pinto beans (about 1/2 pound)
  • 9 cups water, approximately
  • Salt to taste if desired
  • ½ cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon safflower, corn or peanut oil
  • 2 teaspoons finely chopped, fresh jalapeno peppers, seeded
  • ½ cup finely diced, cored, unpeeled tomatoes
  • 6 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander

Preparation

  1. Pick over the beans, and wash them well. Put them in a kettle, and add the water. Bring to a boil, partly cover and let simmer 1 hour.
  2. Add salt and half the onion. Continue cooking, uncovered, about 30 minutes longer.
  3. Heat the oil in a small skillet, and add the remaining onion and the jalapeno peppers. Cook briefly until the onion is wilted. Add the tomatoes and coriander, and cook, stirring, about 3 minutes. Scoop out a cup of the beans plus a little of their liquid, and add it to the container of a food processor or electric blender. Blend as finely and thoroughly as possible. Return this to the beans.
  4. Add the tomato mixture to the beans and continue simmering about 5 minutes.
