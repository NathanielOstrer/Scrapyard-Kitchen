Pancetta, Penne, Italian

Fred Plotkin 1 serving

Ingredients

  • Salt
  • 3 ounces penne (or vermicelli)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 ounces pancetta, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated pecorino cheese
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Set a large pot (at least 4 quarts) of cold water to boil. When the water reaches a boil, toss in a pinch of salt. When the water returns to a rolling boil, add the penne; cook until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat the oil in a pan and add the pancetta. Saute until the pancetta is a dark pink (be sure it does not turn crisp and golden).
  3. When the penne are cooked, drain them well (do not rinse) and transfer them to a warm bowl. Add the pecorino and an abundant amount of fresh pepper. Top with pancetta and oil, toss thoroughly and serve.
