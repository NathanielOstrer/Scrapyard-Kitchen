Cinnamon, Dark Brown Sugar, Shrimp

Steven Raichlen 6 to 8 appetizer servings, 4 light main course servings

Ingredients

  • 24 extra-large shrimp (1 1/2 pounds), peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

For the glaze:

  • ½ cup dark brown sugar
  • 1 stick (8 tablespoons) unsalted butter, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • ½ cup dark rum
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon-style mustard
  • 1 tablespoon distilled white vinegar
  • ¼ teaspoon each cinnamon, ground cloves and black pepper
  • Pinch of salt
  • 12 sugar cane swizzle sticks (see note)

Preparation

  1. Place shrimp in a mixing bowl and stir in oil, lime juice, garlic, salt and pepper. Marinate 15 minutes while you prepare glaze and skewers.
  2. Place ingredients for glaze in a saucepan, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium, and simmer until thick and syrupy, 4 to 6 minutes.
  3. Cut swizzle sticks in half widthwise, and cut one end off each piece sharply on diagonal. Resulting skewers should be about 5 inches long and 1/4 inch wide.
  4. Set up grill for direct grilling, and preheat to high. Using a metal skewer, make two starter holes in each shrimp, one toward head end, one toward tail. Insert a sugar cane skewer through holes. Repeat with remaining shrimp.
  5. Brush and oil grill grate. Grill shrimp kebabs until cooked, about 2 minutes a side, generously basting with glaze. Serve any remaining glaze as a sauce on the side.

Tip

  • Sugar cane swizzle sticks are sold at Citarella stores for $3.99 for 10 to 12 sticks. Gourmet Garage stores also carry them, in packages of 8 to 10 sticks for $2.99.
