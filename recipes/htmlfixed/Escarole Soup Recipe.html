Chicken Stock, Escarole, Spaghettini

Moira Hodgson 4 servings Time15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 head escarole (about 1 pound)
  • 1 quart chicken stock
  • Â¼ pound spaghettini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Preparation

  1. Wash the escarole and dry it in a salad drier. Chop the leaves coarsely.
  2. Bring the chicken stock to a boil and add the escarole. Stir and cook for five minutes, or until the leaves are wilted and tender. Turn down heat and remove the escarole with a slotted spoon. Set aside.
  3. Bring the stock to boil. Add the spagghetini and cook until al dente (three to four minutes), stirring with a fork to prevent the strands from sticking together. Turn down heat, add the escarole, olive oil, salt and pepper.
  4. Pour the beaten egg in a thin stream into the soup so that it forms strands in the liquid. Serve the soup immediately, passing the cheese separately.
