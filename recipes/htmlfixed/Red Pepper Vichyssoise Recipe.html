Chicken Stock, Potato, Red Pepper, French

Barbara Kafka 4 servings Time1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds red bell peppers, seeded and cut into quarters
  • 5 medium shallots, peeled and minced
  • 1 large baking potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 ⅓ cups chicken stock
  • 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream

Preparation

  1. Place peppers in a steamer over 1 inch of water in a wide-mouthed pot. Cover pot, bring water to a boil and then lower heat to a simmer. Cook for 45 minutes or until peppers are very soft.
  2. Pass peppers through the medium disk of a food mill, or puree in a food processor and then force through a sieve. Reserve.
  3. Place shallots, potato and stock in a small saucepan. Cover.
  4. Bring to a boil. Lower heat, and simmer, covered, for 25 minutes, or until potato pieces are tender when pierced with the tip of a knife.
  5. Combine potato mixture with pepper puree, salt and cream. Puree in 2-cup batches in blender until smooth.
  6. Refrigerate at least 2 hours. If refrigerating overnight, remove from refrigerator about 30 minutes before serving.
