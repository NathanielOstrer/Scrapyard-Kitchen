Asparagus, Pasta, Prosciutto, Italian

Pierre Franey 4 or more servings Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • ¾ pound cut ziti (zititagliata)
  • 5 plum tomatoes, about 1 pound, cores removed
  • 24 asparagus spears, about 1 1/2 pounds
  • ¼ pound very thin slices of prosciutto
  • Salt to taste if desired
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon finely minced garlic
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • ½ cup coarsely chopped fresh basil
  • ¼ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Preparation

  1. Bring 4 quarts of water to a boil for cooking the ziti and vegetables.
  2. Cut the tomatoes in 1/2-inch cubes. There should be about 3 cups.
  3. Scrape the ends of the asparagus spears and cut off and discard about 2 inches of the tough bottoms. Cut off and reserve the asparagus tips, each about 2 inches long. Cut the center portion of the asparagus on the bias into 1-inch pieces. There should be about 4 cups of asparagus, including the tips.
  4. Stack the prosciutto slices and cut them in 1/4-inch-wide strips. There should be about 1 1/4 cups.
  5. Drop the asparagus pieces into the boiling water and cook 3 minutes. Scoop out the asparagus and set aside. Allow the water to continue to boil.
  6. To the boiling water add salt and the ziti. When the water returns to a boil cook the ziti to the desired degree of doneness, about 12 minutes or longer. Scoop out and reserve Y cup of the cooking liquid. Set aside.
  7. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a large skillet and add the garlic. Cook briefly, stirring, and add the prosciutto, stirring. Add the tomatoes, salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, about 3 minutes. Add the cream and bring to a boil. Drain the ziti, add it to the prosciutto mixture and stir. Add the asparagus pieces and the basil. Add the reserved Y cup of cooking liquid. Stir to blend and sprinkle with cheese. Toss and serve.
