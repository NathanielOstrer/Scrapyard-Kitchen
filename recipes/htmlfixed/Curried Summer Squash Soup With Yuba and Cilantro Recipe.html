Curry, Summer Squash, Yuba, Indian

Daniel Patterson Serves 4 to 6 TimeAbout 30 minutes

Dwight Eschliman for The New York Times. Food Stylist: Daniel Patterson.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced lemongrass
  • Salt
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 pounds (about 5 medium) summer squash, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 sheet yuba, cut into pieces about 1/4 inch by 1/2 inch
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro leaves

Preparation

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the oil, onion, ginger, lemongrass and a pinch of salt. Place over medium-low heat and sauté until the onion is tender. Add the curry powder and stir for 2 minutes.
  2. Add the squash, coconut milk and 3 cups of water. Season with salt. Cover and simmer until the squash is tender. Allow the soup to cool until no longer steaming. Working in batches, purée in a blender until smooth.
  3. Pass the soup through a fine-meshed strainer set over the saucepan and return to medium-low heat. Add the yuba and bring to a simmer. Remove from heat, stir in the cilantro and adjust salt to taste.
