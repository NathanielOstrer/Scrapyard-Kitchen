Apple, Banana, Chili, Malaysian

Richard Flaste About 3 1/2 cups Time1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces dried cascabel chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 4 ounces dried New Mexican or guajillo chilies (or use twice the given amount of ancho chilies, below), stemmed and seeded
  • 2 ounces dried chipotle chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 4 ounces dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 quarts water
  • ½ pound Roma tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, roasted and peeled
  • 1 ¾ cups diced fresh pineapple
  • ½ pound ripe bananas
  • 1 large green apple (Granny Smith or pippin), peeled, cored and chopped
  • 1 ½ teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon cider vinegar
  • Pinch ground clove
  • ¼ teaspoon ground allspice
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 tablespoons peanut oil

Preparation

  1. In a cast-iron skillet, or in an oven at 250 degrees, dry-roast the chilies for 5 minutes, being careful that they don't blacken. Shake once or twice.
  2. Place the chilies and water in a large saucepan and cover; simmer slowly for 30 minutes over medium heat to rehydrate. Drain, reserving 1 cup of water and the chilies; let cool.
  3. Place the tomatoes in a skillet over medium-high heat and let blacken, about 5 minutes. Place the chilies, tomatoes and remaining ingredients except for the oil in a blender; puree. If the mixture becomes too dry, add a little of the reserved chili water. Put through a strainer.
  4. Heat the oil in a high-sided skillet until it just begins to smoke. Add the sauce and cook for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring continuously, to heat through. Serve over grilled meats or fish.
