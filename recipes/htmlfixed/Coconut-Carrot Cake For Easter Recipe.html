Carrot, Coconut, Flour

Florence Fabricant 8 servings

Ingredients

  • Butter for greasing pan
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 ½ cups finely grated carrots
  • 1 ½ cups sweetened grated coconut
  • ½ cup cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • Jelly beans or marshmallow chicks and rabbits for decoration

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350. Butter two eight-inch layer-cake pans, cut circles of wax paper to fit the bottoms, then butter the paper and place in pan bottoms. Dust pans with flower.
  2. Beat eggs with sugar till thick and light.
  3. Mix carrots with one-half cup of the coconut and oil, fold into the egg mixture. Stir in lemon juice.
  4. Sift flour, baking powder, cinnamon and salt, fold into batter. Divide batter evenly between the two pans.
  5. Bake about 45 minutes, until lightly browned and a cake tester comes out clean. Place pans on racks to cool for about 10 minutes.
  6. Remove cakes from pans and allow to continue cooling. Peel wax paper off the cakes.
  7. While the cakes are baking, whip the cream. Transfer it to a very fine mesh strainer or a strainer lined with cheesecloth that will hold it comfortably and suspend it over a bowl. Cover the whipped cream with plastic wrap and refrigerate it until just before serving. This allows excess fluid to drain out of the whipped cream so it will hold its shape better. It can stay refrigerated for several hours.
  8. Just before serving fold half the remaining coconut into the whipped cream. Frost the tops and sides of the layers and stack them. Sprinkle the remaining coconut over the top and decorate with Easter candies.
