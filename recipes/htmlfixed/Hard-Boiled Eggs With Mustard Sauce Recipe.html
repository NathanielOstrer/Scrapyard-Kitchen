Dijon Mustard, Egg, Garlic

Jacques Pepin 6 servings Time15 minutes

Ingredients

For eggs:

  • 6 large eggs

For mustard sauce:

  • 2 large cloves garlic, peeled, crushed and chopped (2 teaspoons)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon-style mustard
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon wine vinegar
  • ⅓ cup canola or safflower oil

For assembly:

  • 12 lettuce leaves

Preparation

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to boil in a saucepan. Lower the eggs into the boiling water. Bring the water back to a very gentle boil, and cook the eggs for 8 to 9 minutes. Drain off the water, and shake the pan to crack the shells of the eggs. Then, add ice and water to cover, and let the eggs cool completely.
  2. Meanwhile, make the mustard sauce by combining all the sauce ingredients except the oil in a bowl. Add the oil slowly, mixing it in with a whisk or a spoon. Set aside at room temperature until ready to use.
  3. Shell the eggs, and cut them in half lengthwise. The center of the yolks should be slightly soft. Divide the lettuce leaves among six plates, and place two halves cut side up on top of the lettuce on each plate. Coat the eggs with the mustard sauce, and serve immediately.
