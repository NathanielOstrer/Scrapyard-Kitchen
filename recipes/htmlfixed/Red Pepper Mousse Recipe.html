Molly O'Neill Four servings Time40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 large red bell peppers, seeded, deveined and cut into large dice
  • 1 small clove garlic, peeled and bruised
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • ½ teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground pepper, plus more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons white-wine vinegar
  • 2 cups yogurt cheese

Preparation

  1. Put the peppers, garlic, thyme, salt and pepper in a nonstick skillet. Sprinkle with the vinegar, partly cover and cook over medium-low heat until the peppers are tender but not soft, about 25 minutes.
  2. Puree the mixture in a food processor or blender until smooth. Strain through a sieve lined with cheesecloth. Fold in the yogurt cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste. This mousse can be stored in the refrigerator in a closed container for up to 3 days. Serve with grilled bread or as a base for pasta salad.
