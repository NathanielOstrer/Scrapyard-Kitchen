Ingredients

For each serving

  • ¼ cup amaranth
  • ½ cup water
  • Pinch of salt
  • ⅓ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • ½ small or 1/4 large apple, grated

Preparation

  1. Heat a small saucepan over medium-high heat and add the amaranth seeds. Shake them in the pan until they begin to smell toasty and a few seeds begin to pop, about 2 minutes. Immediately pour in the water. Do not wait too long because the line between toasted and burnt amaranth is a thin one – as soon as you smell the toasty aroma and see a few seeds popping, add the water. Add the salt, bring to a boil, reduce the heat to low and cover. Simmer 10 minutes.
  2. Stir in the milk and bring back to a simmer, stirring. Cover and simmer another 10 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the amaranth is tender. Remove from heat and stir in the maple syrup. Transfer to a bowl, sprinkle the grated apple on top and serve.

Tip

  • Advance preparation: You can make more than you need and keep in the refrigerator for a couple of days. Amaranth reheats well. I add a little more milk when I reheat.
