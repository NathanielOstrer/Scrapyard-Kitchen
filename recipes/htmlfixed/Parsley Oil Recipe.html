Raw, Extra Virgin Olive Oil, Parsley

Molly O'Neill Three cups

Ingredients

  • 3 bunches flat-leaf parsley
  • 3 cups extra-virgin olive oil

Preparation

  1. Blanch the parsley, stems intact, for 10 seconds. Drain and cool under cold water and dry on paper towels. Place in a blender along with 1 cup of the olive oil and blend completely.
  2. Pour the paste into a clean glass jar, add the remaining oil, shake well, cover tightly and refrigerate for 1 day. The herbs will settle to the bottom of the jar. Arrange a coffee filter over another glass jar and use a ladle to carefully pour the parsley oil into the filter. The draining will take forever. The oil can then be used in a vinaigrette, in cold soup or to garnish chicken or fish. It will keep in the refrigerator for 1 week.
