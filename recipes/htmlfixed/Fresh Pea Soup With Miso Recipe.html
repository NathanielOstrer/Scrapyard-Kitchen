Pea

David Tanis 4 to 6 servings Time30 minutes

Karsten Moran for The New York Times

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon coconut or vegetable oil
  • 1 medium leek, diced, both white and tender green parts
  • ¾ pound sugar snap peas, trimmed, then chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • 4 cups hot chicken broth or dashi
  • 4 tablespoons white or red miso
  • 4 ounces soft tofu
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions
  • A few shiso (perilla) leaves, roughly chopped (optional)

Preparation

  1. Put oil in a heavy saucepan over medium-high heat. Add leek and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add snap peas to pot and season well with salt and pepper. Add 1 cup water and simmer until peas are soft, about 3 minutes. Add broth and miso and cook 1 minute more.
  3. Purée mixture in a blender, then pour through a fine mesh sieve, pressing with a wooden spoon to extract all liquid. Return strained soup to pot and check seasoning. (May be made up to a day ahead and refrigerated. Reheat just before serving.)
  4. To serve, spoon a little tofu into each small soup bowl. Pour hot soup over. Garnish with scallions and shiso.
