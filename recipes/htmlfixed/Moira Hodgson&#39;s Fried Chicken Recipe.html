Chicken, Flour

Moira Hodgson 4 servings Time45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 3-to-4-pound frying chicken, cut up
  • Flour for dredging
  • 4 to 6 tablespoons corn oil
  • 4 to 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Pat chicken pieces dry with paper towels. Place flour (about 1 cup) in brown bag. Lightly dredge chicken pieces with flour, shaking off excess.
  2. Heat oil and butter in 12-inch cast-iron or heavy skillet (or use 2 smaller skillets). You should have 1/2 to 3/4 inch of fat. Add chicken pieces without crowding and fry over high heat for 5 minutes. Turn and fry 3 minutes more.
  3. Cover, turn down heat and cook for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove lid, turn up heat to crisp chicken, about 2 minutes. Drain on paper towels and serve.
