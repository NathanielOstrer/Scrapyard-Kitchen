Vegetarian, All Purpose Flour, Cumin, Sun Dried Tomato

Molly O'Neill 12 servings TimeAbout 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 3 ¼ cups plus 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • ¼ pound unsalted butter, softened
  • 6 oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, well drained
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Combine the flour and 1 1/2 teaspoons of the salt in a bowl, add the water and stir until the mixture forms a dough. Turn out onto a lightly floured surface and knead on a lightly floured surface until smooth. Place in a clean bowl, cover and let stand 1 hour.
  2. Meanwhile, puree the butter, the remaining salt, the tomatoes, the cumin seeds and pepper in a food processor. Set aside.
  3. Divide dough into 12 pieces. Place on a floured surface and flatten each piece with the palm of your hand. Cover all but one piece with plastic wrap and set aside -- do not stack. Roll out the remaining piece into a 5 1/2-inch circle. Spread with a rounded half-teaspoon of the butter mixture.
  4. Roll up tightly, like a jelly roll, making a long strip. Starting at one end, tightly coil the strip, like a pinwheel. Repeat with the remaining dough, refrigerating the finished ones on a baking sheet lined with parchment if your kitchen is warm.
  5. Heat a large cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium heat. Working in batches, cook breads until browned and cooked through, about 1 1/2 to 2 minutes per side. Cut into quarters and serve hot.
