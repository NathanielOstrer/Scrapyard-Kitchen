All Purpose Flour, Ground Beef, Sesame Seed, Korean

Craig Claiborne about 24 dumplings Time1 hour 10 minutes

Ingredients

The dough:

  • ½ cup flour
  • Salt to taste if desired
  • ¼ teaspoon peanut, vegetable or corn oil
  • 3 tablespoons cold water, approximately

The filling:

  • ⅛ pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon grated onion
  • ¼ teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
  • Salt to taste if desired
  • Freshly ground pepper to taste

Preparation

  1. Put the flour into a mixing bowl. Add the salt and peanut oil, and start blending while gradually adding the water. Blend well to make a stiff but pliable dough. If necessary, add a bit more water. Knead well about 5 minutes. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate 30 minutes or longer.
  2. Combine the ingredients for the filling and blend well.
  3. To make dumplings, cut the dough in half. Roll it out on a lightly floured surface into a a very thin circle of the largest diameter possible.
  4. Using a round cutter such as a small biscuit cutter about 1 1/2 inches in diameter, cut out about 24 circles.
  5. Rub the outer rim of one circle of dough with a little water. Fill the center of the round of dough with about 1/2 teaspoon or less of the filling. Fold the circle of dough over to enclose the filling. Pinch around the edges to seal in the filling.
  6. Bring together the pointed outer ends of each half circle of dough and seal together with a drop of water, pressing lightly.
  7. These dumplings are ready to cook although they may be frozen and may be cooked frozen. Drop them into boiling salted water and cook about 2 minutes. Do not overcook. Drain and set aside to use in sin-sul-lo.
