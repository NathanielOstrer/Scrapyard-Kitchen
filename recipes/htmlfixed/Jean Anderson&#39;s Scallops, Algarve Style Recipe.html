Bay Scallop, Garlic, Lemon Juice

Moira Hodgson 8 first-course servings or 4 main-course servings

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bay scallops
  • 2 medium garlic cloves, peeled and minced
  • 4 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ¼ cup fresh coriander, minced

Preparation

  1. Stir-fry the scallops and garlic in the oil in a large, heavy skillet over high heat for three to four minutes -- just until they begin to color and have released their juices. With a slotted spoon, place the scallops in a bowl and reserve them.
  2. Add lemon juice and cayenne to the skillet and boil with the scallop juices about two minutes until they have reduced to a glaze.
  3. Return the scallops to the skillet together with any juices that may have accumulated in the bowl and toss a minute or two over high heat.
  4. Add the coriander, toss again and serve.
