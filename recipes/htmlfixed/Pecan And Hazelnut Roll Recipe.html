Vegetarian, Egg, Hazelnut, Pecan

Marialisa Calta Twelve to fourteen servings Time40 minutes

Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter for greasing the pan
  • 1 cup hazelnuts
  • 1 ¼ cups pecans
  • 9 eggs, separated
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • Dash of salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa
  • ⅓ cup superfine sugar
  • 3 tablespoons Grand Marnier
  • ¼ cup sifted confectioners' sugar
  • 1 ounce shaved unsweetened chocolate

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Grease a jelly-roll pan and line it with wax paper.
  2. In a nut grinder or a food processor, grind separately the hazelnuts and the pecans, pulsing six to eight times. (Do not overprocess, as the mixture can become buttery.) You should have about one and one-quarter cups of pecan flour and one cup of hazelnut flour.
  3. Beat the egg yolks with the brown sugar until light-colored and thick. Fold in the nut flours, baking powder and salt. Add the vanilla.
  4. Whip the egg whites until stiff and fold them into the nut mixture. Spoon into the prepared pan.
  5. Bake for about 20 minutes or until lightly browned. Set aside to cool.
  6. In a mixing bowl, beat the cream with a whisk or an electric beater until thick. Add the cocoa and continue beating until the mixture forms stiff peaks. Fold in the superfine sugar and Grand Marnier.
  7. Place a damp towel over the cake, covering the whole surface. Carefully invert the cake onto the towel and remove the pan and the wax paper.
  8. Spread the chocolate-Grand Marnier filling evenly over the surface. Very carefully and gently, roll the cake up like a jelly roll, starting at the long side. Use the towel to help in the rolling, and roll directly onto a serving platter. Decorate with the confectioners' sugar and shaved chocolate. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
