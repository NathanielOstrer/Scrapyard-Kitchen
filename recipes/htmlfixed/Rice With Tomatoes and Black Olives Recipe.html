Low Fat, Black Olives, Long Grain Rice, Tomato

Marian Burros 2 servings Time25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 6 ounces whole onion or 5 ounces chopped onion (ready-cut) (1 1/3 cups)
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 6 large Italian, French or Greek olives
  • 1 ¼ pounds ripe field tomatoes
  • Basil
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, optional
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Preparation

  1. Combine rice and 2 cups of water in heavy-bottomed saucepan. Bring to boil; reduce heat and cook a total of 17 minutes, until rice is tender and water has evaporated.
  2. Meanwhile chop the whole onion; sauté onion in hot oil in nonstick skillet.
  3. Pit and cut up olives; wash, trim and coarsely chop tomatoes. When onion is tender add the olives and tomatoes and cook over high heat to evaporate liquid.
  4. Wash, dry and chop basil to make 2 tablespoons. Just before adding tomato mixture to rice, stir in basil. Add mixture to cooked rice and season with optional salt and with pepper.
