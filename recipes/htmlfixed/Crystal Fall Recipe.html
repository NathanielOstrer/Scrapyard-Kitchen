Apple Cider, Cognac, Rum

Robert Simonson 1 drink

Evan Sung for The New York Times

Ingredients

FOR THE SIMPLE SYRUP

  • 1 cup sugar

FOR THE DRINK

  • 1 ½ ounces Cognac, preferably Pierre Ferrand 1840
  • ½ ounce Lemon Hart Demerara rum
  • ¼ ounce palo cortado sherry, preferably Lustau
  • ¼ ounce ginger syrup (brands available at Whole Foods and many other stores include the Ginger People and Morris Kitchen, of Brooklyn)
  • ¾ ounce fresh apple cider
  • ½ ounce lemon juice
  • 1 dash Angostura bitters
  • Apple slice

Preparation

  1. Make the simple syrup: Heat the sugar in 1 cup water until sugar is dissolved. (Can be stored in refrigerator.)
  2. Mix the drink: In a shaker half-filled with ice, combine 1 teaspoon simple syrup and the Cognac, rum, sherry, ginger syrup, cider, juice and bitters. Shake, then strain over crushed ice into a double rocks glass. Garnish with apple slice.
