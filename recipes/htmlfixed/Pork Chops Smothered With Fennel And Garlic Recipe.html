Dairy Free, Nut Free, Fennel, Garlic, Pork Loin Chop, Southern

Sarah Belk Four servings Time45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
  • 4 center-cut loin pork chops, 1 inch thick
  • 4 to 8 cloves garlic, peeled and slivered
  • 1 ¼ cups chicken broth
  • ⅔ cup dry white wine
  • 2 fennel bulbs, trimmed, quartered, and cut into very thin slices
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • Salt, freshly ground pepper

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 225 degrees.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Pat chops dry and add to skillet. Cook 5 to 6 minutes on each side or until deep golden brown. Pour off fat.
  3. Remove skillet from heat (leave pork chops in skillet) and add garlic. Cook 1 minute in the hot skillet.
  4. Return skillet to heat and add 1 cup broth, the wine and fennel, and bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer covered 5 minutes.
  5. Remove chops to a pie plate or other ovenproof dish and cover lightly with foil (set skillet aside). Place chops in the oven and cook 10 to 15 minutes, or until a meat thermometer registers 160 degrees when placed near the bone.
  6. Meanwhile, simmer the sauce in the skillet 5 minutes or until reduced slightly.
  7. In a small cup or bowl mix remaining 1/4 cup broth (it should be cold or room temperature) with the cornstarch. Add this mixture to the sauce and boil 1 minute, or until lightly thickened. Taste, adding salt and pepper, if desired. Serve chops "smothered" with sauce.
