Brandy, Plum

Matt Lee And Ted Lee 2 quarts Time1 hour

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds large purple plums (about 12)
  • 1-inch-long piece of cinnamon stick
  • 2 cups sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups brandy

Preparation

  1. Boil a stockpot of water as deep as the shoulder of a 2-quart jar with hinged lid.
  2. Wash and stem plums. Prick several holes around stem ends. Pack plums in jar. Quarter and pit plums that don't fit in jar and re-pack, placing quartered pieces in gaps that form between whole plums. Add cinnamon stick.
  3. In a 2-quart saucepan or skillet, bring sugar, salt and 1 cup water to a boil, then turn to low and simmer 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Let cool for 10 minutes, stir in brandy. Immediately pour the liquid into jar up to 1/2-inch from the rim.
  4. Partly close jar, to leave gap for steam to escape, and place in pot of boiling water for 10 minutes. Carefully remove jar with jar lifter or 2 tongs and close the lid tightly. Cool to room temperature. Refrigerate for up to 2 weeks.
